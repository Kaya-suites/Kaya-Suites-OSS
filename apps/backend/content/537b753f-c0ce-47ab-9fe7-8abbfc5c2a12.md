## 1. Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

Before any outreach, the team must be aligned on **who** we're targeting. This avoids wasted effort and ensures high conversion rates.

| Criteria | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | SMBs in professional services, e-commerce, healthcare, or real estate (adjust as needed) |
| **Company Size** | 5–100 employees |
| **Pain Signal** | Manual, repetitive workflows; data entry bottlenecks; disconnected tools; scaling challenges |
| **Budget Range** | $2,000–$15,000 per project / $500–$3,000/mo retainer |
| **Decision Maker** | Founder, COO, Operations Manager, or Head of IT |
| **Tech Stack Fit** | Uses tools we integrate well with (e.g., Zapier, Make, n8n, Airtable, HubSpot, Google Workspace, Slack) |

> **Best Practice:** Revisit the ICP quarterly. Track which client types yield the highest LTV and lowest churn, then refine.

## 2. Acquisition Channels (Ranked by ROI for Small Teams)

A 2–3 person team cannot be everywhere. Focus on **2–3 high-leverage channels** at a time.

### 2.1 Warm Referrals & Partnerships (Priority #1)
- Ask every satisfied client for **1–2 referrals** at project close.
- Build referral partnerships with complementary service providers (web designers, marketing agencies, bookkeepers).
- Offer a referral incentive: e.g., 10% credit on next invoice or a flat $250 bonus per signed deal.

### 2.2 LinkedIn Outbound (Priority #2)
- Each team member sends **5–10 personalized connection requests/day** to ICP contacts.
- Follow the **3-touch framework:**
  1. **Connect** – Short, friendly note (no pitch).
  2. **Value** – Share a relevant insight, case study, or quick audit (48–72 hrs later).
  3. **Invite** – Offer a free 20-min "Automation Opportunity Audit" (5–7 days later).
- Use a lightweight CRM or spreadsheet to track pipeline status.

### 2.3 Content & SEO (Priority #3 – Long-Term)
- Publish **2 pieces/month**: case studies, "before & after" automation breakdowns, or short how-to guides.
- Post natively on LinkedIn; repurpose to blog/website.
- Optimize website for keywords like *"business process automation for [industry]"*.

### 2.4 Optional / Situational Channels
- **Cold Email:** Use only with verified, permission-based lists. Keep volume low and personalization high.
- **Communities:** Engage in Slack/Discord communities, Reddit, or industry forums where ICP hangs out.
- **Events/Webinars:** Host a monthly 30-min "Automation AMA" to generate inbound leads.

## 3. Sales Process (Simple 4-Stage Pipeline)

Keep the pipeline lean. A small team needs speed and clarity, not complexity.

```
┌──────────────┐    ┌──────────────┐    ┌──────────────┐    ┌──────────────┐
│  1. LEAD IN  │ →  │ 2. DISCOVERY │ →  │ 3. PROPOSAL  │ →  │  4. CLOSED   │
│              │    │    CALL       │    │   & FOLLOW-UP│    │  (Won/Lost)  │
└──────────────┘    └──────────────┘    └──────────────┘    └──────────────┘
```

### Stage 1 – Lead In
- Source: inbound inquiry, referral, or outbound response.
- **Action:** Respond within **2 hours** during business hours. Speed-to-lead is the #1 conversion lever.
- Log in CRM with source, contact info, and initial pain point.

### Stage 2 – Discovery Call (20–30 min)
- **Goal:** Understand the prospect's workflows, pain, and desired outcomes.
- **Framework (S.P.I.N.):**
  - **S**ituation – What does your current process look like?
  - **P**roblem – Where are the bottlenecks / frustrations?
  - **I**mplication – What does this cost you in time/money/errors?
  - **N**eed-Payoff – If we automated X, what would that unlock for you?
- End with: *"Based on what you've shared, I'm confident we can help. I'll send over a tailored proposal within 48 hours."*

### Stage 3 – Proposal & Follow-Up
- Send a **clear, concise proposal** (1–3 pages max) covering:
  - Problem summary
  - Proposed solution & automations
  - Timeline (typical: 1–4 weeks)
  - Investment (fixed-price preferred for trust-building)
  - What's included / what's not
- **Follow-up cadence:** Day 2 → Day 5 → Day 10 → Day 20 (then archive if no response).

### Stage 4 – Close
- Use a **digital contract + e-signature** tool (e.g., PandaDoc, HoneyBook, or even a simple Google Doc + Stripe link).
- Collect **50% upfront** before work begins (best practice for small teams to protect cash flow).
- Send a welcome email with onboarding steps and timeline.

## 4. Key Metrics to Track

You can't improve what you don't measure. Track these **weekly** in a shared dashboard or spreadsheet.

| Metric | Target (Starting Benchmark) |
|---|---|
| **Outreach Volume** | 50–75 touches/week (combined team) |
| **Discovery Calls Booked** | 3–5/week |
| **Proposals Sent** | 2–4/week |
| **Close Rate** | 25–40% of proposals |
| **Average Deal Size** | $3,000–$8,000 |
| **Time to Close** | < 14 days from first contact |
| **Client Acquisition Cost (CAC)** | Track hours spent × blended hourly rate |
| **Referral Rate** | ≥ 30% of new clients from referrals |

> **Best Practice:** Hold a **15-min weekly pipeline review** (Mon or Fri) where each team member shares: new leads, stuck deals, and closes.

## 7. Quick-Reference: Objection Handling

| Objection | Suggested Response |
|---|---|
| *"It's too expensive."* | "Let's look at the ROI. If this saves your team X hours/week at $Y/hr, it pays for itself in Z weeks." |
| *"We can do it in-house."* | "Absolutely — if you have the bandwidth. Most clients come to us because they want it done faster and maintained long-term." |
| *"We're not ready yet."* | "Totally fair. Can I send you a quick audit so you have a roadmap when you are ready?" (Keeps the door open.) |
| *"Can you guarantee results?"* | "We scope every project around measurable outcomes. I'll include specific KPIs in the proposal so we're both aligned." |

## 8. Monthly Review Checklist

- [ ] Review pipeline: any deals stale > 21 days? Re-engage or archive.
- [ ] Update ICP based on last month's wins/losses.
- [ ] Collect and publish at least **1 testimonial or case study**.
- [ ] Audit acquisition channels: double down on what's working, pause what's not.
- [ ] Celebrate wins 🎉 — small teams run on morale.
